About Me

A Ph.D. student with an expertise in microeconomics, economics of crime and economic policies. My research examines the degree to which changes in crime rates in both the US and England and Wales can be explained by business cycles, using a panel data analysis. A member of the leading economist team in an externally funded project that includes the Police Knowledge Fund investigating cybercrimes. Research assistant in the newly formed Centre for Crime, Justice and Policing at the University of Birmingham, while being the research consultant at the Warwickshire Police and West Mercia Police, in an effort to help them understand cybercrimes. Easy going by nature and strong team player; always willing to take initiatives. Presently seeking to expand my knowledge and skills, by achieving a job in Consultancy.
